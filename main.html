Note 1

Jesus Christ, the central figure of Christianity, is considered the Savior of mankind and the Son of God. According to Christian beliefs, he was born in Bethlehem, Judea, approximately 2000 years ago and spent his life guiding people to the path of mercy, humanism, and moral principles. His life and teachings are recorded in the New Testament, which is one of the main holy books of Christianity.

Note 2

Jesus Christ taught people about God the Father and His love for each person. He emphasized the need for mercy and forgiveness, taught that you should care about your neighbor, even if he is your enemy. He spent a lot of time with different people, visited the sick and healed them, even raising the dead Lazarus, which was one of his miracles.

Note 3

In the Christian tradition, Jesus Christ was crucified on a cross and died as a sacrifice for the sins of humanity. However, on the third day after his death, he rose from the dead. This was an important event that confirmed his divinity and led to the creation of the Christian religion. In the Christian tradition, Jesus Christ is an exponent of peace, love, and moral principles that people learn to follow in their daily lives.
